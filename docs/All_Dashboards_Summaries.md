# All Dashboards Summaries

__1\.Medicaid Claims — Dashboard Summary__

Illinois | Indiana | Kentucky  •  Apr–Oct 2025

The Medicaid Claims Dashboard covers pharmacy claims data across three states — Illinois, Indiana, and Kentucky — from April to October 2025, with a total of 336 claims, members, and providers and an average claim amount of $169\.68\. Claims are analyzed across four Managed Care Organization \(MCO\) plans \(PlanA Health, PlanB Managed, PlanC Care, PlanD Plus\) and five eligibility groups \(Adult, Child, Aged/Blind/Disabled, Expansion Adult, and Pregnant Women\), all of which show a nearly equal distribution of claims\. The data is also split between two delivery models — Fee\-for\-Service \(167 claims\) and MCO \(169 claims\) — where MCO consistently shows higher spend across all states\. GLP\-1 drugs are the dominant drug class throughout the entire dataset, accounting for 45% of all claims \(152 of 336\), with tirzepatide being the highest\-cost drug and semaglutide close behind\. GLP\-1 usage is evenly spread across all three states and all eligibility and MCO groups, with spend peaking sharply in April 2025 at ~$19K and declining to near zero by July\. Geographically, Illinois leads in both total claims \(~125\) and paid amount \(~$20\.5K\), with county\-level maps revealing urban clusters in northern Illinois and central Indiana and clear disparities between urban and rural areas\.

__2\.Foster Care Analytics — Dashboard Summary__

United States  •  FY 2021

The Foster Care Analytics Dashboard covers child welfare data across the United States for FY 2021, tracking 6\.6K children in care at the start of the year, 3\.4K new entries, 3\.6K discharges, and 1\.9K children waiting for adoption\. The Demographics dashboard shows a near\-equal gender split \(51\.6% male, 48\.4% female\), with Non\-Hispanic White \(34\.74%\) and Hispanic or Latino \(30\.64%\) as the two largest ethnic groups\. Most children are placed in major metro areas \(7\.81K\) compared to non\-metro \(1\.24K\) and rural \(880\) settings, and 40\.6% of children had only one placement while 21\.5% experienced four or more placements\. Most children stayed in foster care for 2\+ years \(4\.04K\), and entries significantly exceeded exits in months 7–12 of the fiscal year\. The Ethnicity & Payments dashboard reveals that neglect is the leading reason for removal across all racial and ethnic groups, followed by parental drug abuse and caretaker inability to cope\. Group homes have the highest average monthly payment \(~$4K\), while relative foster homes have the lowest \(~$314\)\. XIX Medicaid is the dominant federal support type, and discharge reason code 99 is the most common across all top states including California \(759\), Texas \(456\), and Florida \(401\)\.

__Clinical and Operational — Dashboard Summary\(AHRQ\)__

__United States •  MEPS HC\-243 Survey  •  5,000 Respondents__

The Clinical and Operational Dashboard is based on a survey of 5,000 respondents and tracks healthcare spending, demographics, utilization, and COVID\-19 impact across the United States\. The Demographics dashboard shows that Seniors \(65\+\) are the largest age group \(1\.19K\), White Non\-Hispanic is the dominant racial group \(2\.71K\), and the South has the highest regional representation \(1\.91K\); private insurance covers 59\.14% of respondents, while 6\.96% are uninsured, and the average total health spend per year is $7,406\.83 with an average out\-of\-pocket cost of $975\.57 and average Rx drug spend of $1,652\.25\. The Amount Spending dashboard breaks down healthcare expenditures by age group, insurance type, race, and region, highlighting how spending patterns vary significantly — seniors and those on public insurance tend to have higher total costs, while out\-of\-pocket and prescription drug costs differ across racial and ethnic groups\. The Utilization and COVID dashboard examines healthcare service usage — including office visits, hospital stays, and emergency visits — alongside COVID\-19's impact on care patterns, showing how the pandemic disrupted regular utilization and increased public insurance enrollment across multiple demographic groups\.

__DCS Foster Care Funding — Dashboard Summary __

United States  •  DCS Foster Care •  Power BI

The DCS Foster Care Funding Dashboard \(Power BI\) covers funding sources and cost breakdowns for 10,000 children in foster care across the United States\. The Funding dashboard shows a total estimated cost of $313\.67M, with an average monthly payment of $1\.05K per child; children with disabilities cost significantly more at $1\.68K per month versus $1\.06K for non\-disabled children\. Delaware leads all states with the highest average monthly payment \($3\.6K\), followed by Maryland \($2\.69K\) and Tennessee \($2\.51K\), while Title XIX Medicaid is the fastest\-growing funding source over reporting months, rising well above Title IV\-E Foster Care Payments and Title IV\-D Child Support Funds\. Clinically diagnosed disabilities account for the highest monthly disease\-related spend \($4\.0M\), followed by emotional disturbance \($3\.2M\) and other medical conditions \($2\.0M\)\. The Ethnicity Funding dashboard breaks down funding sources by race, showing that Medicaid is the dominant funding source across all racial groups — covering 75\.9% for Asian children, 67\.54% for Alaskan/American Native, 67\.06% for White, and 65\.5% for Black/African American children — while IV\-E Foster Care is the second largest source and SSI remains consistently small across all groups at under 5%\.

__Agency for Health Care Research and Quality\(AHRQ\) Dashboard — Summary__

United States  •  MEPS HC\-243  •  Power BI

The AHRQ Dashboard \(Power BI\) is based on the MEPS HC\-243 survey and covers general demographics, health status, and disease\-related expenditures across the United States\. The General dashboard shows that 87\.52% of interviews were conducted in English, White Non\-Hispanic is the largest racial group \(1,413 females, 1,294 males\), and High School Diploma holders are the most common education level \(1,332 born in the USA\); employed respondents overwhelmingly hold private insurance \(1,704\), while those not in the labor force rely more heavily on public insurance \(698\), and key health indicators show 6\.96% uninsured, 15\.7% with mental health needs, 5\.4% with a long COVID rate, and 10\.3% with activity limitations\. The Diseases and Status dashboard reveals total healthcare expenditure of $37\.03M across all regions, with the South generating the highest spend \($12\.49M\) and the Northeast the lowest \($6\.53M\); nearly 50% of respondents \(2\.5K\) do not have hypertension, while 29\.88% \(1\.49K\) do, and the majority of emergency room visits are single visits \(45\.11%\), though a small number of individuals account for as many as 14 visits\. Married respondents have the highest food stamp receipt counts, followed by never\-married individuals, highlighting the intersection of social support needs and health spending across different population segments\.
